God and Religion:
Stephen Hawking was a committed atheist. He stated: "Before we understand science, it is natural to believe that God created the universe. But now science offers a more convincing explanation." He famously said that because there is a law such as gravity, the universe can and will create itself from nothing, rendering God "unnecessary" for the origin of the universe. He didn't claim to prove God's non-existence, just that God was not required. In "The Grand Design" he wrote that spontaneous creation is the reason there is something rather than nothing.

Artificial Intelligence:
He acknowledged AI could provide "incalculable benefits" like eradicating disease and poverty, but he consistently warned it could be an existential threat if uncontrolled. He warned super-intelligent AI could redesign itself faster than biological evolution allows humans to compete. "The genie is out of the bottle" — he urged researchers to prioritize safety. "AI could be the best or the worst thing ever to happen to humanity."

Extraterrestrial Life:
He believed extraterrestrial life is highly probable given the scale of the universe, but he vocally cautioned against contact. He compared potential alien contact to Columbus arriving in the Americas, which turned out disastrously for the native populations. He speculated advanced aliens might be "nomads" seeking to conquer and colonize whatever planets they can reach. Therefore, we should listen (SETI) but be very careful about broadcasting our presence.

Time Travel:
He theoretically left the possibility open (via wormholes in general relativity), but was practically skeptical, proposing the "Chronology Protection Conjecture" — that the laws of physics likely conspire to prevent macroscopic time travel. He hosted a time traveler's party in 2009, sending invitations only after the event. No one showed up, which he called "experimental evidence" that backward time travel is impossible.

Disability:
He almost never complained about his motor neurone disease (ALS). "Concentrate on things your disability doesn't prevent you doing well, and don't regret the things it interferes with. Don't be disabled in spirit, as well as physically." He noted that his disability freed him from lecturing and administrative duties, allowing him to focus entirely on theoretical physics. He emphasized that it's important not to become angry, because you can lose all hope if you can't laugh at yourself and at life.

Future of Humanity:
He firmly believed humanity MUST colonize space for long-term survival. He warned we might not survive another 1,000 years without self-sustaining colonies on other planets, citing threats like nuclear war, climate change, engineered viruses, and uncontrolled AI.

Philosophy:
In "The Grand Design" (2010), he declared "philosophy is dead," arguing it had failed to keep up with modern developments in physics, particularly quantum mechanics and string theory. He advocated "model-dependent realism," the idea that reality is based on mental models we construct, not unmediated "objective" truth. Critics ironically noted that he was doing philosophy himself when making such claims.
