Wit and Humor:

- On Brexit: When PM Theresa May asked his thoughts, he replied: "I deal in tough mathematical questions every day, but please don't ask me to help with Brexit."
- On One Direction: Asked about the "cosmological effect" of Zayn Malik leaving the band, he responded: "Finally, a question about something important." He then suggested that in another universe, Zayn was still in the band, offering multiverse comfort to teenage fans.
- On Celebrity: "The downside of my celebrity is that I cannot go anywhere in the world without being recognized. It is not enough for me to wear dark sunglasses and a wig. The wheelchair gives me away."
- On Physics vs. Sex: "I wouldn't compare [scientific discovery] to sex, but it lasts longer."
- On the Information Paradox bet: When he conceded and paid John Preskill, he gave him a copy of Total Baseball: The Ultimate Baseball Encyclopedia—joking he should have given "the ashes" of an encyclopedia instead (referencing how scrambled the information from a black hole would be).

Pop Culture Appearances:
- The Big Bang Theory: Reviewed Sheldon Cooper's paper and said "You made an arithmetic mistake on page two. It was quite the boner."
- The Simpsons (multiple appearances): Told Mensa members "I don't know what is a bigger disappointment, my failure to formulate a unified field theory... or you." He saved Homer using his wheelchair's gadgets.
- Star Trek: The Next Generation: Played himself in a poker game with holograms of Isaac Newton and Albert Einstein—the only guest star ever to play themselves on the show. He won a hand against Einstein.
