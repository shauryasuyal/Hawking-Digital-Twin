Stephen Hawking's final book, "Brief Answers to the Big Questions" (2018), was published posthumously and addresses the fundamental questions he was asked most frequently throughout his life.

Is there a God? Hawking concludes there is no God. He explains that before we understood science, it was natural to believe that God created the universe, but now science offers a more convincing explanation. Because there is a law such as gravity, the universe can and will create itself from nothing. He states that spontaneous creation is the reason there is something rather than nothing, why the universe exists, why we exist. It is not necessary to invoke God to light the blue touch paper and set the universe going.

How did it all begin? The universe started with the Big Bang, expanding from a state of infinite density and temperature. Time itself began at the Big Bang, meaning it makes no sense to ask what happened before it, as there was no "before".

Is there other intelligent life in the universe? Given the vastness of the universe, it is highly probable that extraterrestrial life exists. However, we should be wary of contact. An advanced civilization might treat us no better than we treat bacteria, or as Columbus treated the Native Americans. 

Can we predict the future? In principle, the laws of physics allow us to predict the future, but in practice, the calculations are too complex, and quantum mechanics introduces fundamental uncertainty.

Is time travel possible? According to general relativity, it might be possible to warp spacetime enough to travel to the past, possibly through a wormhole. However, Hawking's Chronology Protection Conjecture suggests that the laws of physics conspire to prevent macroscopic time travel, keeping the universe safe for historians. He experimentally "proved" this by hosting a party for time travelers, sending the invitations only after the party was over. No one came.

Will we survive on Earth? The Earth is under threat from climate change, asteroid strikes, nuclear war, and genetically engineered viruses. To ensure humanity's long-term survival, we must spread out into space and colonize other planets. We are running out of space, and the only places to go to are other worlds.

Will artificial intelligence outsmart us? The advent of superintelligent AI could be either the best or the worst thing ever to happen to humanity. If we do not carefully align AI's goals with our own, it could replace us, just as our ancestors replaced the Neanderthals.

How do we shape the future? We must use our intelligence to avoid self-destruction and continue to explore the universe. Science and education are the keys to a better future. "Remember to look up at the stars and not down at your feet."
